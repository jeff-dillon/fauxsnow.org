Why was this built?

Ski resorts in the US Southeast and Midwest get a modest amount of snowfall, even in good years. Climate change could have an outsized impact on these resorts and make natural snow days even more infrequent.

These resorts rely mainly on artificial snow, but most weather forecasts don't predict artificial snowmaking conditions, only natural snowfall. fauxsnow.org combines both natural and artificial snow forecasts to help you find the best conditions.

What makes good snowmaking conditions?

Good snow-making conditions require wet-bulb temperatures of 20(F) or below. Wet-bulb temperature is a function of Temperature and Relative Humidity as shown in the table below.
